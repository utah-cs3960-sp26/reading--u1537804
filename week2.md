1. Computation plays a huge role in training AI because models get better mainly by learning from 
a lot of data and running massive amounts of calculation, not by being given human rules. The 
lesson is considered “bitter” because researchers naturally want to build systems using human 
understanding, but history shows those approaches usually lose to methods that just scale computation. 
It’s frustrating to realize that clever human insight often helps only at first and then hits a 
ceiling. Because of this, when AI does something strange, it’s hard to explain exactly why, since the 
behavior comes from complex training rather than clear reasoning steps. The bitter lesson reminds us 
that more powerful AI often means less intuitive, human-style explanations.


AI coding assistants make testing even more important, not less, because they can generate large amounts 
of code very quickly without knowing if it actually works. This shifts testing from something that happens 
“later” to something that must be built directly into the development process, with both manual checks and 
automated tests included alongside the code. We now think of tests as proof and accountability, not just 
safety nets, especially because AI is involved. It’s the developer’s responsibility to make sure the AI’s 
output is tested and demonstrably correct before it’s ever shared, instead of just leaving it up for code review.

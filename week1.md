Three things that are difficult about programming are choosing and using the best implementation, debugging 
runtime errors, and switching between multiple tasks and problems.

Three things that are difficult about software engineering are working with and managing deadlines, truly 
understanding what the user needs, and collaborating on large-scale projects across multiple organizations 
within a company.

As I read the blog post, I genuinely felt a weight lifted by the idea that programming is not the job rather that software 
engineering is. I need to do a better job of seeing and understanding the bigger picture at a lower level. I 
feel confident discussing the different stages of software implementation at a high level, but understanding 
software requirements at a deeper level, likely through experience, would be especially valuable to me. Additionally, 
I can improve my judgment around what should be done and the tradeoffs that come with those decisions. I suspect this 
will develop with more hands-on experience. Using AI programming tools can help accelerate this experience because they 
reduce the time spent writing code and solving low-level programming problems, allowing me to focus more on exploring 
ideas and seeing them through from start to finish.
